Simple Linear Regression

 

Simple linear regression is useful for finding relationship between two continuous variables. One is predictor or independent or explanatory variable and other is response or dependent variable. It looks for statistical relationship but not deterministic relationship. Relationship between two variables is said to be deterministic if one variable can be accurately expressed by the other. Relationship between two variables is said to be deterministic if one variable can be accurately expressed by the other. Mathematically it is represented as

Y = a + bX

Here, Y is the response variable and X is the independent variable, b is the coefficient and a is the intercept.

Statistical relationship is not accurate in determining relationship between two variables. The core idea is to obtain a line that best fits the data. The best fit line is the one for which total prediction error for all data points are as small as possible. Error is the distance between the point to the regression line.
